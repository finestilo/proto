We look at InVerto "Prototype 00", the only steel prototype of the CIC InVerto 39 Titanium and the stage before the watch was finalized.
Prototype #00
The watch before the InVerto Titanium
Image © Phillips Auction House
Lederer InVerto “Prototype 00” is the only existing prototype watch of the CIC InVerto 39 Titanium architecture. It is a steel execution of the watch and sits outside the regular production. Fully Functional, yet distinct from the final watch that followed. It belongs not to the collection but to the process that made it possible.
To understand why it exists, one has to begin with what InVerto set out to do.
2023- Lederer Timepieces introduced CIC InVerto 44 mm.
The idea behind this timepiece was direct: make the movement more understandable, expose the architecture more, and let the time read through the mechanics. Let the logic of the movement speak for itself.
In 2025, Lederer Timepieces introduced InVerto Titanium 39, the successor to the 44 mm version with improved proportions, a thinner and lighter grade 5 titanium case, and better spatial balance.
The idea proved compelling. As of March 2026, Inverto Titanium is fully allocated for 2026-2027, with inscriptions now extending into 2028.
The logic behind InVerto was clear, but it did not begin with inversion.
It began earlier with the realization that the movement had outgrown its position. When the Central Impulse Chronometer stopped being something that sits behind the dial. When it stopped being just another component of the watch and became something that defined the timepiece.
With this realization, the question was not only how to improve it but also how to place it.
The shift was not neutral, as the mechanics were no longer separated behind the dial — they were the dial.
Engineering could resolve how to build it — through redesign, structural refinement, or spatial rebalancing. But there was another question that engineering alone could not answer: whether it could hold once it became real.
Because when you invert a watch like this, you are not just flipping it, but you are also risking how the watch is read. You are risking the legibility of the watch. Does the eye read time first or the mechanism? Does the clarity hold, or does it give way to complexity?
These questions are not resolved in theory, but they are resolved in form.
This is where Prototype 00 belongs, representing the phase in watchmaking where everything is under scrutiny.
Most prototypes do their work quietly; they resolve problems, help finalize the components, and once the watch is complete, they disappear.
This one did not.
What makes this different is not the rarity, not the material difference. It exists because it didn’t simply answer if the system worked — it did. But it also answered whether the watch remains coherent when its logic is fully exposed with reduced scale and redistributed visual weight.
Most watches are encountered long after these uncertainties are gone and the decisions are final. This prototype preserves a moment in watchmaking that is not commonly seen; it preserves the moment before the watch became one. Where nothing is final but everything is present.
The InVerto “Prototype 00” offers a glance into the period of watchmaking when the watch is still under evaluation.
