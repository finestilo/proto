This Week
We explore InVerto “Prototype 00”, a unique steel prototype preserving the decisive moment before the final timepiece reached its definitive form — now presented at Phillips Auction Geneva.

LEDERER InVerto “Prototype 00” is the unique prototype of the CIC InVerto 39 architecture, a steel execution that stands outside regular production, yet at the very origin of the final watch. It captures the decisive moment when Bernhard Lederer’s vision was fully revealed, tested, and affirmed. Not merely an earlier version, but the turning point in the making of InVerto.
And that is precisely why it stands apart.
This piece was not conceived to be preserved. It existed to answer questions. To be observed, adjusted, and reconsidered. For a time, it remained close to Bernhard Lederer himself. It was handled, studied, and worn as the architecture revealed its strengths and its limits. It is within this intimate process that the watch found its truth.
The last moment before resolution
Executed in steel and existing outside the regular production sequence, it occupies a rare position: outside the collection, yet at its origin.
This is not an earlier version. It is the moment at which the final watch began to take shape.
When Lederer introduced the CIC InVerto 44 mm in 2023, the intention was clear: “A movement should not be hidden. It should be allowed to speak.” The goal was not to create an effect, but to make the movement more intelligible, to reveal its architecture, and to allow time to be read directly through the mechanics. In other words, to let the logic of the caliber speak for itself.
This pursuit led to a deeper realization. The Central Impulse Chronometer had outgrown the space it was meant to occupy. It was no longer something to be hidden behind a dial but something to be fully assumed. It had become the defining element of the watch.
From there, the question was no longer only how to refine it, but how to position it.
Inversion was not a decorative choice. It was a structural one. Once the movement becomes the dial, the usual separation disappears. The mechanism is no longer behind the display; it becomes the display.
Engineering could resolve the construction, the proportions, and the spatial balance. But it could not answer the more subtle question: would the watch remain harmonious when fully exposed?
Would the eye read time immediately, or be overwhelmed by the mechanics? Would clarity endure, or would complexity dominate? These were not abstract concerns. They had to be resolved in form.
It belongs to that rare stage in watchmaking where nothing is yet final, but everything already matters. It is the piece in which the architecture was tested, not in theory, but in reality, on the wrist, in motion, under scrutiny.
Most prototypes disappear once their role is complete. This one endured because it preserved something essential: the last open chapter before the watch was settled.
Here, Bernhard Lederer had not yet committed to the definitive form. Proportions could still evolve. Hierarchies could still be reconsidered. The balance between technical expression and immediate legibility was still being determined.
When the InVerto 39 Titanium was introduced, that decision had been made. The case was refined, reduced, and executed in grade 5 titanium. The architecture reached a level of clarity and equilibrium that confirmed the strength of the concept. Production is fully allocated for the year ahead, with waiting lists continuing to expand.
But the final watch belongs to resolution. Prototype 00 belongs to the moment just before it.
For the collector, this distinction is decisive. The great pieces are not only those that are rare, but those that preserve a meaningful chapter in the making of the work. Here, rarity is matched by narrative depth: the record of a watch still in the process of becoming itself.
"Prototype 00" is not merely a precursor. It is the turning point.
It carries the tension between doubt and conviction, between possibility and decision, between the idea and the form. It captures a moment that cannot be revisited. It holds something that a non-finished piece can never contain again.
And that is why it stands apart.
